Essential Travel Tips for Japan

Prepare for your Japanese adventure with practical travel tips, from transport and etiquette to budgeting and packing.

Getting Around Japan

Japan’s transportation is quick and easy to navigate. The Shinkansen (bullet train) is the fastest way to travel between cities like Tokyo, Osaka, and Kyoto. In the cities, metro systems are straightforward, and you'll find signs in English. If you're traveling long distances, the Japan Rail Pass is a great way to save money. For shorter trips, buses and taxis are also available, though they can be a bit pricier.

Japanese Etiquette

In Japan, being polite is essential to making a positive impression. A simple bow is the proper way to greet people, especially in formal settings. When you enter someone’s home or a temple, it’s important to take off your shoes. Tipping is not practiced in Japan and can even be seen as disrespectful. While dining, keep your voice down, and remember to say “Itadakimasu” before eating and “Gochisosama deshita” when you're done. Lastly, always be mindful of public spaces—talking loudly or using your phone in public is frowned upon.

Weather Tips

Japan experiences distinct seasons, each with its own charm. Spring is mild, ideal for cherry blossom viewing. Summer is hot and humid, often with sudden rain showers. Autumn offers cooler temperatures and vibrant foliage, while winter can be cold, especially in northern areas. Be sure to pack accordingly to enjoy the weather comfortably.

Packing Tips

When packing for Japan, think about the climate and your planned activities. During the summer (June to August), pack light, breathable clothing to stay cool in the heat and humidity. In winter, especially in Hokkaido, temperatures can drop, so bring warm layers. Japan is known for its onsens (hot springs), so don’t forget a small towel for those visits. Also, make sure to carry a power adapter, as Japan uses 100V outlets with a Type A plug.
